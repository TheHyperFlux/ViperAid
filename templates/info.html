Snake Safety Information

Preventing Snakebites

  • Wear boots and thick pants when walking in grassy or forested areas.
  • Use a flashlight when walking outdoors at night.
  • Avoid sticking your hands into holes, thick grass, or under rocks without checking.
  • Keep your living area clean and free of debris to deter snakes.
  • Store food properly and avoid leaving it outside—it can attract rodents, which attract snakes.

What to Do if Bitten

  • Stay calm and try to remember the color and shape of the snake.
  • Keep the affected limb immobilized and lower than the heart.
  • Remove tight clothing or jewelry near the bite area.
  • Do not attempt to suck out the venom or apply ice.
  • Call emergency services or use ViperAid’s rescue tool to get help.

Handling Snake Encounters

  • Do not try to catch or kill the snake—it may provoke an attack.
  • Slowly back away and give it space to escape.
  • If the snake is inside your house or won’t leave, use ViperAid’s rescue feature.
  • Educate others not to harm snakes unnecessarily—they are vital for ecosystems.

Common Snakes in Nepal

Nepal is home to several venomous and non-venomous snakes, including:

  • Indian Cobra – Highly venomous, hooded, found in lowlands.
  • Krait – Active at night, neurotoxic bite, slender body.
  • Russell’s Viper – Aggressive, highly venomous, triangular head.
  • Rat Snake – Non-venomous, often mistaken for cobras.
